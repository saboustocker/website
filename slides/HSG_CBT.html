A Specification Curve Analysis of NLP Classifiers Diagnosing Psychological Mechanisms Influencing Decision-Making

Master’s Thesis Sabou Rani Stocker
Cognitive and Behavioral Decision Research
University of Zurich
supervised by Prof. Dr. Renato Frey and Olivia Fischer

Background

  • many proposed state-dependent influences,
    little research on their occurence in real life

    Lob, Fischer, and Frey (2025), Holleman et al. (2020), Quinan et al. (2015)

  • natural language as data and NLP as method

    Binz et al. (2025), Ostrovsky and Newell (2024),

  • promising first results but no systematic evaluation

    Heitz, Schneider, and Langer (2024), Fischer, Lob, and Frey (2024), Bhatia (2024), and others

Aim & Research Question

Aim

Systematic comparison of algorithms harnessing NLP on their capabilities and limitations in diagnosing state-specific mechanisms influencing people when making decisions

Research Question

How reliably can we identify state-specific mechanisms from natural language influencing people in decision-making?

Methods

Data Collection

  • online experiment, \(n\) = 320

  • randomized-controlled study: four groups
    (baseline, 3 manipulations)

  • simultaneous performance of think-aloud protocols

Implications

Methodological

  • methodological approach \(\geq\) mechanism

Practical

  • viable to test (co-)occurrence of mechanisms in naturalistic scenarios
